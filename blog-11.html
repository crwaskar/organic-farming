The Real Reason Why Organic Food is Expensive

Organic food is grown by farmers who priorities the use of renewable resources and soil and water conservation in order to improve the environment for future generations. Most traditional insecticides and fertilizers generated with synthetic chemicals or sewage sludge, bioengineering, or forms of radiation are not used in the production of organic food. Before a product can be branded "Organic," a government-approved certifier inspects the farm where it is grown to ensure that the farmer is adhering to all USDA organic regulations.

The neurological, endocrine, and immunological systems of young children and developing foetuses may be harmed by average quantities of nitrate (a common fertilizer) and a pesticide in groundwater, according to a 1994 study from the University of Wisconsin. A 1973 study linked high levels of sodium nitrate in groundwater to the prevalence of gastric cancer, and a 1996 study linked it to the prevalence of testicular cancer.
