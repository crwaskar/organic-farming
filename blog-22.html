Organic Farming - The Environmentally Friendly System

Organic farming has been receiving growing attention in the recent years, as more and more people recognize the importance of protecting our planet and sustainable growth. In this article, you will discover how organic farming differs from other methods of agriculture, and why it’s such an effective and environmentally friendly system for producing crops. So let’s start with the definitions...

Organic farming can be described as an environmentally friendly farming system that doesn't use chemical-based pesticides, artificial fertilizers or genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Although it's one of the most efficient ways to grow food, it was only introduced commercially in the 1940s, and in the US, legislation only came about in 2002. But organic farming is still far from reaching its full potential worldwide.
