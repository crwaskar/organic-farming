Sustainability Starts on the Farm: Advantages of Organic Farming

Organic farming is one of the most important sustainable practices on the farm today, and as consumers become more environmentally conscious, demand for organic products will continue to rise. If you’re thinking about switching over to an organic farm or just want to know more about the benefits of this practice, these are the top five advantages of organic farming you should know about.

For organic farmers, sustainability isn’t just something that happens at the end of the day or in a few months—it’s something they work towards every day, from the seeds they plant to the harvest they gather. Organic farming not only helps keep soil fertile and water clean, but it also benefits farmers, consumers, and communities around the world by reducing environmental damage and protecting human health. By making sustainable choices such as choosing organic over conventional foods, people can play their part in keeping the planet green! Here are some of the top advantages of organic farming that support sustainability.

Although organic farming still represents only a small percentage of total agricultural production in the United States, it’s quickly growing in popularity as more and more people turn to it to support their health and the environment. Organic farmers don’t use harmful pesticides or herbicides and they don’t use GMOs (genetically modified organisms) or antibiotics on their crops, making their farms natural, sustainable places where wildlife can flourish and farmers can grow food while reducing their impact on the planet.
